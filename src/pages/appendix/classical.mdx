At last, here is the puzzle the Italian mathematician Paolo Guarini di Forlì set
down in 1512 — the one that started this whole line of thought.

The board is the same 3×3 grid, but the starting position is different: two
**red** knights sit across the top row, and two **blue** knights across the
bottom. The goal is different too. Guarini did not want the colours merely
interchanged row-for-row. He asked for the knights to be rearranged into a
particular pattern: the two blue knights on one diagonal of the board, and the
two red knights on the other.

Take your time. Try short sequences and long ones. If you find yourself
retracing your steps, that is not a sign that you are losing — quite the
opposite. The patterns you notice, and the patterns you *cannot* seem to find,
are both telling you something about how the nine squares are wired together.
On the next page we make that wiring explicit, and the puzzle dissolves.
